# IQHouse Blog Content Strategy

## Overview
IQHouse blog (Insights) focuses on **B2B thought leadership** for educational brand buyers and OEM/ODM partners. All content should be professional, methodology-driven, and avoid parenting/consumer-focused topics.

---

## Three Approved Article Types

### Type 1: How Learning Concepts Become Physical Toys
**Purpose:** Demonstrate design methodology and expertise

**Example Topics:**
- How we translate "spatial reasoning" into a tangible puzzle design
- From classroom observation to product prototype: A case study
- Designing for hand-eye coordination: The design thinking process
- Why physical interaction matters more than digital alternatives

**Tone:** Educational, process-focused, professional
**Audience:** Brand buyers, educators, OEM/ODM partners
**Length:** 800-1200 words

**Structure:**
1. Hook: Real classroom observation
2. Problem: Why traditional approaches fall short
3. Solution: Our design methodology
4. Example: Specific product case
5. Takeaway: Why this matters for brands

---

### Type 2: OEM vs ODM: What Brands Should Consider
**Purpose:** Help buyers understand partnership models and make informed decisions

**Example Topics:**
- OEM vs ODM: Key differences and when to choose each
- Design copyright and intellectual property in toy manufacturing
- Quality assurance in OEM partnerships: What to look for
- Timeline and MOQ considerations for educational toy production
- Customization vs. efficiency: Finding the right balance

**Tone:** Informative, advisory, professional
**Audience:** Brand procurement teams, distributors, retailers
**Length:** 900-1400 words

**Structure:**
1. Define OEM and ODM clearly
2. Compare key differences (cost, control, timeline, IP)
3. Discuss decision factors (brand needs, budget, timeline)
4. Highlight IQHouse's approach
5. Call to action: Schedule consultation

---

### Type 3: Why Hands-On Learning Still Matters
**Purpose:** Reinforce the value proposition of physical educational toys

**Example Topics:**
- The neuroscience of hands-on learning vs. screen time
- Why 12 learning capabilities require physical interaction
- Design thinking for educational toys: A methodology overview
- How original gameplay design drives learning engagement
- The role of tangible products in early childhood development

**Tone:** Evidence-based, thoughtful, professional
**Audience:** Educational brands, distributors, institutional buyers
**Length:** 800-1200 words

**Structure:**
1. Challenge: Why digital alone isn't enough
2. Evidence: Research on hands-on learning
3. Framework: The 12 learning capabilities
4. Application: How IQHouse designs for this
5. Conclusion: Future of educational toys

---

## ❌ Topics to AVOID

- **Parenting advice** ("How to help your child develop...")
- **Age-specific content** ("Best toys for 3-year-olds...")
- **"How to play with kids"** guides
- **General child development** (unless directly tied to design methodology)
- **Product reviews** of competitors
- **Trending parenting topics** (screen time, developmental milestones, etc.)
- **Lifestyle content** (family activities, birthday party ideas, etc.)

---

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone & Voice
- **Professional** but accessible
- **Methodology-driven** (explain HOW we think)
- **Avoid self-praise** (don't say "we're the best")
- **Evidence-based** (reference research when possible)
- **B2B focused** (write for business buyers, not parents)

### Structure
- Clear headline that states the value
- 2-3 sentence intro with context
- Logical flow with subheadings
- Concrete examples and case studies
- Professional conclusion with CTA

### Language
- Use "we" sparingly; focus on the topic
- Avoid marketing jargon
- Use specific, concrete language
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)
- Use active voice

### Example Good Headline
❌ "Why IQHouse Toys Are Amazing"
✅ "How Physical Interaction Transforms Abstract Concepts: A Design Methodology"

## Publishing Checklist

Before publishing any article:

- [ ] Follows one of the three approved types
- [ ] Avoids all prohibited topics
- [ ] Professional, methodology-driven tone
- [ ] No self-praising language
- [ ] Clear value for B2B readers
- [ ] Proper English grammar and spelling
- [ ] 800-1400 words (appropriate length)
- [ ] Includes relevant examples or case studies
- [ ] Has clear CTA (contact, learn more, etc.)
- [ ] Bilingual version ready (EN + ZH)

---

## SEO Considerations

Each article should include:
- **Meta description:** 150-160 characters, value-focused
- **Keywords:** 2-3 primary keywords (e.g., "educational toy design," "OEM manufacturing")
- **Internal links:** Link to relevant pages (Capabilities, OEM & ODM, etc.)
- **External links:** Reference credible sources when applicable

---

## Questions to Ask Before Writing

1. **Is this about our design methodology or business model?** (Yes = write it)
2. **Would a brand buyer care about this?** (Yes = write it)
3. **Does this help readers make better decisions?** (Yes = write it)
4. **Is this parenting/consumer advice?** (Yes = DON'T write it)
5. **Does this sound like self-promotion?** (Yes = reframe it)
